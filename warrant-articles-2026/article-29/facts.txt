ARTICLE 29: Tiered Operating Override

Quick facts
- Category: Override
- Sponsor: Select Board / Finance Director
- Vote threshold: Majority
- Key: FY27 contingent appropriation up to $4.3M; trash override 7-2
- Stakes: high
- Recommendation: favorable (FinCom)
- Result: tbd

Journalist notes (tracker)
- Article 29 is the main town-side override article and the central vehicle for the new three-tier, three-year operating override structure. The Select Board decided to anchor the override package here rather than treat it as just another supplemental line item. The article is expected to contain the contingent FY27 appropriation of roughly $4.3M, representing year one of the highest tier (Tier 3), while the June 9 ballot would ask voters to choose among the broader $9M, $12M and $15M cumulative override frameworks. In other words, Article 29 supplies the legal authority for the FY27 town-side piece and explains how later-year draws would fit into the pyramid structure if voters approve a tier. Officials said the exact legal text and detailed breakdown were still being refined with town counsel as of the April 22 meeting so the Town Meeting vote, ballot questions and multi-year draw schedule stay aligned. That refinement is still pending, but the policy decision to anchor the overrides in Article 29 has already been made. If the override fails at the ballot, this article is void.
- 
- At the April 27 hearing, FinCom voted to recommend the overall town-and-school operating override (excluding the trash override) and to appropriate up to $4.3 million in year one as summarized for Town Meeting. FinCom took a separate vote on the curbside collection/trash override component, which passed 7-2. Mark the trash component as favorable, 7-2.
