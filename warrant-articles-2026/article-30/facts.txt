ARTICLE 30: Rescind Gerry/Brown bond authorization

Quick facts

- Vote threshold: Majority
- Key: $1,619,627 of $54.8M bond
- Stakes: low
- Recommendation: favorable (FinCom)
- Result: tbd

Journalist notes (tracker)
- When a town authorizes borrowing for a construction project, any unspent portion must be formally rescinded once the project is done. The Gerry Elementary School renovation (the building is now called Brown Elementary) was approved at $54.8M. The project is complete with $1,619,627 unspent, so this article retires that leftover authorization. Housekeeping — no new money is being appropriated or spent. FinCom voted to recommend rescission.

Primary source (warrant text)
ARTICLE 30: Rescind Bond Authorization 
To see if the Town will vote to rescind the $1,619,627 portion of the $54,844,767 borrowing 
authorization approved by the Town on May 7, 2019 (Article 51) and voted by ballot question on 
June 18, 2019 for the design and construction of a new Elementary School (Gerry Elementary 
School) that has not been used and that is no longer needed for such purpose; or to take any 
other action relative thereto. Sponsored by the Finance Director.
