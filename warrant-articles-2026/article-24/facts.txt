Journalist notes (tracker)
- The stabilization fund is the town’s rainy-day reserve — a restricted savings account that requires a two-thirds vote of Town Meeting to draw from. The current balance is $1.5M, roughly 1.4 percent of the operating budget. The state Department of Revenue recommends 5 percent and the town’s own financial policies call for 3 to 5 percent. No new contribution is proposed for FY27 given the budget constraints, and nothing is being withdrawn. FinCom made no recommendation since there is no amount to act on.
