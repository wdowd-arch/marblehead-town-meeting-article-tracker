ARTICLE 11: Walls and fences

Quick facts
- Category: Capital
- Sponsor: Select Board
- Vote threshold: Majority
- Key: $25,000 from taxation (cut 50 percent from $50K)
- Stakes: low
- Recommendation: favorable (FinCom)
- Result: tbd

Journalist notes (tracker)
- Funds construction and repair of walls and fences that protect highways and property — in a coastal town, that mostly means sea walls. Historically funded at $50,000, cut in half this year as part of the budget crunch. DPW has outstanding sea wall projects at Front Street, the causeway (both sides) and Ocean Avenue; a design evaluation alone is $80,000. Unspent funds carry over year to year. DPW says one year at $25K is manageable but the cut compounds going forward, delaying critical coastal infrastructure work that could eventually require a debt exclusion. FinCom voted to recommend the $25,000 appropriation.

ARTICLE 11: Walls and Fences 
To see if the Town will vote to raise and appropriate a sum of money for the construction and 
reconstruction of walls and fences for the protection of highways and property, including 
engineering services in connection therewith; to authorize the appropriate Town Officers to 
acquire by purchase, eminent domain or otherwise, any land or easements necessary therefore; 
to determine whether this appropriation shall be raised by borrowing or otherwise, or take any 
other action relative thereto. Sponsored by the Select Board.
