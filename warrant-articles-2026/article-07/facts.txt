ARTICLE 7: Revolving funds

Quick facts
- Category: Finance

- Vote threshold: Majority
- Key: $4,205,300 max spending cap
- Stakes: low
- Recommendation: favorable (FinCom)
- Result: tbd

Journalist notes (tracker)
- Revolving funds let certain departments spend the fees they collect without going back to Town Meeting each time — but state law requires voters to set a maximum spending cap annually. This article sets those caps for 13 funds. Largest:
-  Park $2M
-  Commercial Waste $1.3M
-  COA $350K
-  Special Ed $200K
-  Street Opening $200K
-  Conservation Fines $75K FinCom increased the waste cap from the proposed $1,067,000 to $1.3M to provide a risk cushion as the town transitions to the new annual curbside fee. Total is down $994K from FY26, driven by a waste construction project rolling off. FinCom voted to recommend the $4,205,300 spending cap.

ARTICLE 7: Departmental Revolving Funds 
To see if the Town will vote to fix the maximum amount that may be spent during FY 2027 
beginning July 1, 2026, for the revolving funds established in the town bylaws for certain 
departments, boards, committees, agencies or officers in accordance with G.L. c. 44 § 53E ½, or 
take any other action relative thereto. Sponsored by the Select Board.
