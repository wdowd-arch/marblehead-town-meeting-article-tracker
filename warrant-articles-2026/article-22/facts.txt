ARTICLE 22: Available funds to reduce tax rate

Quick facts
- Category: Budget
- Sponsor: Finance Director
- Vote threshold: Majority
- Key: $5,360,000 ($5M free cash + $360K electric surplus)
- Stakes: medium
- Recommendation: favorable (FinCom)
- Result: tbd

Journalist notes (tracker)
- Each year, the town uses non-tax revenue sources to offset the property tax levy so the tax rate does not have to cover the entire budget. ‘Free cash’ is the audited surplus left over after the prior fiscal year closes; the electric light department also generates a surplus. This year the town is using $5M in free cash and $360K from electric surplus — a total of $5.36M. That’s down $2M from the $7M used last year, and that $2M reduction is one of the biggest drivers of budget pressure because it eats into the annual property tax growth. After this appropriation plus $288K for lease-purchase, roughly $700K-$1.2M in free cash remains as an emergency cushion. Town Administrator Thatcher Kezer says reducing reliance on free cash for the operating budget is a policy priority — when he arrived three and a half years ago, the town was using $10M. FinCom voted to recommend the $5,360,000 appropriation.
