Why this matters to Article 39
- The story describes Article 39 as a citizen petition to repeal a prior Town Meeting vote (Article 34 on the 2024 warrant) that established the Community Development and Planning Department.

Key facts/claims from the piece
- The department is described as having been established by Town Meeting in 2024; in early 2025, the Select Board appointed the first director of planning and community development.
- Kuker argues the department concentrates too much authority in an unelected office under the town administrator and that the planning board (elected) should be the “proper home” for a town planner.
- The piece quotes Kuker objecting to enabling language he reads as overly broad (“including but not limited to”).
- Proponents (as characterized in the story) argue the department was created by democratic vote and consolidates previously fragmented functions.

How to use this in Article 39 writeups
- Use as attribution for the petition’s rationale and the main lines of debate: consolidation/efficiency vs accountability/board control.
