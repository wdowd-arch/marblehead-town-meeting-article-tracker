Why this matters to Article 38
- The story describes Article 38 as a citizen petition to return the Select Board to one-year terms (annual elections).

Key facts from the piece
- Marblehead had annual Select Board elections for more than 300 years before Town Meeting voted in 2023 to adopt three-year staggered terms via a home-rule petition.
- The change was signed into law by Gov. Maura Healey in March 2024.
- A 2024 Town Meeting attempt to undo the switch (Article 49) is described as failing 121–236; a similar effort in 2021 also was defeated.
- Kuker argues one-year terms increase accountability (voters can weigh in on all five members each year, rather than usually two).
- Supporters of three-year terms argue continuity/insulation from short-term pressure and note reversal would be unusual after legislative approval.

How to use this in Article 38 writeups
- Use the vote-history numbers and timeline (2023 vote → March 2024 law → 2024 reversal attempt result) as quick context for readers.
