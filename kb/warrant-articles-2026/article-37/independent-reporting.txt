Why this matters to Article 37
- The story profiles William Kuker and describes Article 37 as one of three citizen petitions he sponsored aimed at “curbing town power.”

Key claims/description from the piece (summarized)
- Article 37 is described as limiting employment contracts for municipal positions to one calendar year, excluding collective bargaining agreements.
- The article quotes Kuker’s rationale: shorter contracts create closer scrutiny/oversight and accountability for administrators.
- The piece notes critics’ warning that one-year contracts could hurt recruitment in a competitive municipal job market; Kuker disputes that concern.

How to use this in Article 37 writeups
- Use as attribution for intent/motivation and to frame the pro/con arguments likely to come up on the floor (accountability vs recruiting/stability).
