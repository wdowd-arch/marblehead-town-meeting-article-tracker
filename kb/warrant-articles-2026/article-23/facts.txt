ARTICLE 23: Expenses of Several Departments

Quick facts
- Category: Budget
- Sponsor: Finance Director
- Vote threshold: Majority
- Key: $109.78M general fund / $122.77M total
- Stakes: high
- Recommendation: favorable (FinCom)
- Result: tbd

Journalist notes (tracker)
- The main event — the annual operating budget that funds every department in town. This is the no-override baseline: the amount the town can spend within its existing Proposition 2½ levy limit. FinCom voted to recommend $122,762,030 in total appropriations, of which $109,777,938 comes from taxation. Major line items:
-  Schools $47.62M (absorbed 22 position cuts totaling $3.157M in reductions)
-  Health insurance $16.75M
-  Debt service $11.1M
-  Curbside trash collection $2.19M (new line item) If the override fails, this budget stands as-is and the curbside line converts to a roughly $262/household fee. The School Committee voted 4-0 on April 9 to request a three-tier override ($6.2M/$7.2M/$8.5M for schools) above this floor.

To see what sums of money the Town will raise and appropriate, including appropriations from 
Federal Revenue Sharing moneys, to defray the necessary and usual expenses of the several 
departments of the Town for the fiscal year beginning July 1, 2026, or take any other action 
relative thereto. Sponsored by the Finance Director.
