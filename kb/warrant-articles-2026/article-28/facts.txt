ARTICLE 28: School supplemental (override)

Quick facts
- Category: Override
- Sponsor: School Committee
- Vote threshold: Majority
- Key: Folded into Article 29; awaiting School Committee action
- Stakes: high
- Recommendation: pending (FinCom)
- Result: tbd

Journalist notes (tracker)
- This is the school half of the Proposition 2½ override. If voters approve an override at the ballot, this article appropriates additional school funding above the Article 23 baseline. The School Committee voted 4-0 on April 9 to request three tiers:
-  $6.2M at the $9M override level
-  $7.2M at the $12M override level
-  $8.5M at the $15M override level Revenue is split 62/38 between schools and town under a memorandum of understanding that also includes quarterly reporting and a commitment not to seek another override until FY30. Article 29 is the primary town-side article anchoring the override structure and FY27 contingent appropriation. If the override fails at the ballot, this article is void.
- 
- At the April 27 hearing, officials said the schools are not asking for a separate override article in year one. The school portion is expected to be handled through Article 29, the broader supplemental appropriation/override article. The school articles had not technically been indefinitely postponed yet, but the schools were expected to vote on next steps. Treat as pending — folded into Article 29 / awaiting School Committee action.

Primary source (warrant text)
ARTICLE 28: School Department Supplemental Appropriation 
To see if the Town will vote to raise and appropriate or transfer from available funds a sum of 
money to supplement the School Department’s operating budget for fiscal year 2027, contingent 
upon the passage of a Proposition 2 ½, so called, ballot question, or take any action relative 
thereto. Sponsored by the School Committee.
